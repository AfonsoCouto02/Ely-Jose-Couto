Biography

Ely José Couto

Science Fiction Writer & Creative Mind

Ely José Couto is a lost dude with a keen interest in doing interesting things he feels like at the time. Sometimes he gets really deep into Rocket Science. Sometimes it's Physics. But most often it is crazy stories that pop mysteriously in his mind and melodies that come together with them.

Born with an insatiable curiosity about the universe and its mysteries, Ely found himself drawn to the infinite possibilities of science fiction from an early age. His writing explores the intersection of human emotion and technological advancement, often asking the fundamental question: what does it mean to be human in a world where the boundaries between self and other, reality and simulation, are constantly shifting?

When he's not crafting stories about distant worlds and complex characters, Ely can be found diving deep into scientific papers about rocket propulsion, quantum mechanics, or the latest developments in artificial intelligence. This scientific curiosity infuses his fiction with a sense of authenticity and wonder that resonates with readers who appreciate both rigorous science and compelling storytelling.

His work spans multiple formats, from short stories and novellas to full-length novels, screenplays, and even musical compositions. Each piece reflects his belief that the best science fiction doesn't just imagine possible futures—it helps us understand the present moment and our place within it.

Current Project:

Ely is currently seeking representation for his latest novel, "It Crawls Under Your Skin," a psychological science fiction thriller that explores themes of identity, consciousness, and the nature of reality through the story of Sylvia Lake and her relationship with an entity she calls "the Nameless."

Writing Philosophy:

"Science fiction isn't about predicting the future—it's about understanding the infinite possibilities that exist in every moment. Every story I write is an attempt to capture one of those possibilities and share it with someone else who might see the universe a little differently afterward."
